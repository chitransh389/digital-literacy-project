Phishing is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information like passwords or bank details.

In a typical phishing attack, the attacker sends a fake email or message pretending to be from a trusted organization such as a bank. The message contains a link that looks legitimate. When the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website where they are asked to enter personal details.

Students and online users are common targets because they may not always verify links. Once the attacker gets the information, they can misuse it for financial fraud or identity theft.

The consequences of phishing include loss of money, data theft, and privacy issues. It can also cause mental stress to the victim.

Therefore, it is important to stay alert and verify sources before sharing any information online.
